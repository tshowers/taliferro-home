Introduction

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is integral to any marketing strategy. A CRM aims to build a closer relationship with customers than they have with each other or anyone else. It's used to organize contacts, manage relationships with them, and help you keep track of their preferences and history so that you can tailor your marketing efforts appropriately in the future.

What is CRM?

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) is a system that helps you organize your contacts, track your sales and manage your customers. In short: it's a way of managing the relationship between you and those who buy from you.

The importance of a CRM grows as the number of interactions between companies and their customer's increases. With so many channels available to reach out to potential clients, it can be challenging to track where they've come from and what they've done in the past.

CRM helps business owners stay organized by providing a place to store all the information about their customers' preferences, buying habits, and contact preferences.

Benefits of CRM in Marketing

  • CRM helps to understand your customers
  • CRM helps to keep track of customer interactions
  • CRM helps to improve customer experience
  • CRM helps to increase sales
  • It makes it easy to keep track of leads and follow up with them quickly
  • It gives you an overview of all your customers, so you know who they are and what they need throughout their journey with you
  • It helps automate repetitive tasks to free up time for more meaningful work
  • Provides access anywhere at any time on any device

Challenges of CRM in Marketing

CRM is a challenge for marketers because customer data is dispersed across multiple systems and not accurate or available to the right people.

  • Customer data is not accessible: The lack of integration between systems means that even if you have access to all your customers' information, it's not in one place and, therefore, difficult to access. For example, if you have sales data but don't have marketing automation software to analyze it, you won't get the insights needed for your campaigns.
  • Poor customer experience: If your system isn't set up correctly, this can lead to poor customer experiences like missed emails or phone calls. Plus, there are always delays in processing orders—and as we know from personal experience (or just watching TV), customers don't like waiting around.

Introduction

We live in a digital world, and e-commerce is a part of our lives as breathing. According to the e-Commerce Foundation, the number of people who use internet shopping has increased. According to statistics, expect internet shopping to continue rising. This means more people will be shopping online than ever—and they want their experience to be easy and seamless.

Create a simple login process

When creating a simple, secure login process, you need to consider two types of users: people who already have an account and those who don't. For existing users, the ideal login is a passwordless entry method that allows them to enter their accounts and make purchases as quickly as possible. For new users, there should be a simple way to create an account to start buying from your store.

The best solution will depend on what type of product or service you're selling and who your target market is. Passwordless logins are probably unnecessary if you sell one-off products (for example, tickets or event passes) because people will only buy them once. They'll likely remember their passwords well enough that they won't forget them entirely between purchases. Since these purchases are infrequent (often last-minute), removing the barrier of signing up for an account altogether may increase sales.

On the other hand, if your company sells subscriptions or memberships like Netflix, having a passwordless option can significantly improve user experience by speeding up transactions.

A recent survey shows that 48% of shoppers have abandoned an online purchase because they could not log in quickly enough. This is especially problematic for those new to the site and who need to create an account to continue the purchase process.

This high abandonment rate can be attributed to what is known as the "cognitive friction" associated with having users create a username and password combination. The challenge with this approach is that it requires users to remember multiple sets of login credentials. Remembering so many credentials often results in users writing down their passwords on sticky notes and keeping them near their computers or phones, which puts sensitive information at risk if someone else happens.

Passwordless

A passwordless login option allows customers who have previously made purchases on your site a frictionless way into your e-commerce store.

Instead of asking for these credentials over again, you can send them an invitation link via email so that they don't have any difficulty logging into future purchases made from within your app or website.

If your e-commerce site takes more than four clicks of the mouse or ten seconds of time before users can get into their account, you're losing sales. Thirty-five percent of shoppers will abandon a cart for this reason.

The login process should be simple and secure. Don't make it difficult for customers to sign in with their username and password, or they may become frustrated and give up on the purchase altogether.

Personalize the shopping experience

With all your information on your customers, there's no excuse not to personalize the shopping experience. Personalization significantly affects the overall customer experience and can also help boost sales. Here are some ways you can use your customer data to make their shopping experience more enjoyable and memorable:

Personalize based on customer preferences. This is where most e-commerce platforms fall short—they don't offer any way of personalizing the site or product recommendations based on shoppers' previous behavior.

Instead of providing generic suggestions, use your customer data to create a more personalized experience for each person browsing your site by showing them items they're likely to buy based on what they've bought.

Consider similar grouping products so customers can find them quickly instead of floundering through pages and random offerings.

Personalize based on location. Customers who shop from different places have different expectations about shipping costs and delivery times.

While some customers may not mind paying extra for faster delivery times (or even spending more money altogether), others will only buy from stores that offer free shipping or expedited options whenever possible.

Ensure a seamless customer experience

Don't forget your checkout page: make sure it's fast and straightforward.

Ensure that your website is mobile-friendly by having a clear call-to-action on every page of the site and ensuring that navigation links are large enough for people who use their phone's touchscreen navigation.

Online shopping is more than just a hobby or a tool for convenience. It's an integral part of our daily lives, and people have come to expect a seamless experience, whether interacting with an extensive global retailer or a small, local shop.

Conclusion

When e-commerce sites don't meet these expectations, users can become frustrated and feel like their time isn't valued—something no business wants in its customer base.

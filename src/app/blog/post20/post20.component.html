Introduction

The holiday season is approaching, and you're probably trying to figure out how to make your online store stand out from the crowd. The good news is that you don't have to do all of this by yourself: there are plenty of tools, strategies, and tactics out there that will help any new or established retailer grow their business. Here are some tips on turning your fashion and apparel online store into a sales juggernaut this fall.

Gather research on a target audience and relevant influencers

To start, you need to know who your customers are and what they want. You should also know your competitors and how their products differ from yours.

You can use tools such as Google Analytics or Facebook Insights to find these answers. For example, if you're selling clothes online and have an eCommerce store on Shopify, a tool like Google Analytics can tell you who is visiting your site and what they do when they get there (also known as "behavior"). This will help you understand what kinds of content they're looking for while browsing through the site—and whether or not it helps them make purchase decisions. It's also important to look at what influencers in this space say about fashion trends—what colors are on trend? What types of styles are popular right now? Knowing this information can help tailor what kind of content gets published on social media sites such as Instagram or Pinterest.

Create different messaging for different channels

The next step is to create different messaging for different channels. The way that you approach content, customer acquisition, and messaging on each channel will depend on its audience, format, type of content, and potential customers' buying habits. For example:

On Instagram, where your typical follower is a millennial in their 20s or 30s who loves to shop online but mostly buys from luxury brands like Gucci and Louis Vuitton, you should post photos showcasing your products aspirationally (like Kylie Jenner).

On Facebook, where most followers are middle-aged women interested in DIY crafts or interior design projects, you should be sharing tips on how to sell clothing items at flea markets with posts featuring sewing tutorials like "How To Sew Your Own Jumpsuit" or "How To Turn Old Jeans Into A Boho Bag!"

Email marketing software is essential to know who people are and what they've done before signing up for your emails. Do they open all messages? Click any links within them? Or did they sign up without even clicking through once?

Don't wait until the last minute to plan your Black Friday promotions

You don't want to be caught off guard by a surge in demand. This is why it's essential to start planning Black Friday promotions early.

  • Ensure that you have enough inventory to meet your products' increased demand.
  • Make sure you have enough staff available to handle the increased customer traffic during Black Friday (and throughout the holiday season).

Develop a social media strategy that includes giveaways and contests

  • Giveaways and contests are a great way to get people to engage with your brand.
  • Repost photos from the contest on your social media channels.
  • Giveaways and contests can also help build relationships with your customers.

Don't let user-generated content go to waste; put it in your promotion

User-generated content (UGC) is an excellent way for you to engage with your audience and get them excited about sharing their photos or videos of themselves wearing your clothing. Users are more likely to share their content if it's authentic and not too heavily branded, so consider what kind of UGC you might want to include.

Make your website fast, easy to navigate, and responsive

  • Make your website fast. This is a no-brainer, but every second counts when it comes to online shopping. If your site takes too long to load or doesn't load at all, customers will leave and go elsewhere.
  • Use a fast hosting provider and CDN (Content Delivery Network).
  • Reduce HTTP requests as much as possible.

Optimize your pages for mobile search and have a "click-to-buy" feature

When optimizing your website, you should always ensure that it is mobile-friendly. This means that the site is easy to navigate on mobile devices and offers a pleasant experience for shoppers. In addition to ensuring that the website is optimized for mobile users, it's essential to provide a "click-to-buy" feature so customers can order products from their phones or tablet.

Another important aspect of online retail optimization is having an optimized product description page for each item you sell in your store. A great way to do this effectively is by including relevant keywords in your title and description fields - using longtail keywords like "blue shirt" instead of broad terms like "shirt." You can also add links between different sections within the description field so people can easily see what else you have available or related items they might want - all without leaving one specific product page.

Understand how to use the keywords

  • Use the right keywords in your product descriptions and titles, but don't stuff them in there, or you'll look like a spammer. Keywords should be natural and not forced.
  • Know what your competitors' customers are searching for so you can target those same terms with your products. For example, if you have an online store selling baby clothing, you may find that people are searching for "baby clothes" or "baby outfits." So, include these phrases in the titles of some of your products.
  • Look up what other stores in your industry are doing with their product descriptions—this will give insight into what works well for similar businesses online (and keep an eye out for any mistakes).

Start remarketing campaigns early in the fall season so you can identify and engage with potential customers early on

Remarketing is a great way to reach people who have visited your website but haven't made a purchase. It's also one of the most effective ways to generate sales all year. And once you start running a remarketing campaign, you'll see which products convert best and which images perform best when it comes time for A/B testing or optimizing your campaigns.

There are lots of ways to test interests and messaging within remarketing campaigns:

  • Test different images on different audiences (e.g., male vs. female)
  • Test additional messages based on what they've viewed previously (e.g., "Shop now" vs. "Discover more")

Figure out where to spend your budget for digital ads based on your marketing goals and KPIs

Before you start advertising, you need to know your marketing goals and the key performance indicators (KPIs) that will help you measure whether or not those goals have been met. For example: If your goal is to increase store visits and sales, one of your KPIs might be "number of purchases."

You also need to figure out how much money you can spend on digital ads, which depends on your budget. If this is a new business venture for you, it's likely that there won't be much room in the budget for digital ads at first. But some businesses may already allocate a percentage of their revenue toward marketing efforts—in which case they should also consider putting some of their marketing budget toward online ad campaigns. Once all these pieces are in place—the goal(s), the KPI(s), and how much money to spend—you'll be ready to dive into the world of paid media.

This is the time of year when many retailers start to see their sales increase

If you're an online fashion retailer, this is when many retailers start to see their sales increase. It's also an excellent time for you to make that happen too.

Black Friday is an excellent opportunity for online shops because it takes advantage of how people shop: they are on their phones and computers all day, so why not take advantage of that? If you have an online store and want Black Friday to be your best day ever (and who doesn't?), then here are some quick tips on how to get started:

  • Make sure your website looks good on mobile devices.
  • Make sure your website loads quickly and works well at any hour of the day or night.
  • Work on getting reviews from customers through social media channels.

Conclusion

If you're looking to grow your business this holiday season, you must start planning now. Don't wait until the last minute and expect everything to fall into place for you. Take some time this week and think about all the different ways you can market yourself and your products online—and then do them. You'll be glad you did when those sales start pouring in later.

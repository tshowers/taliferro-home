What is a Laboratory Information System?

Lab Information Systems (LIS) are software applications that automate the lab workflow. These systems can help manage laboratory operations, automate business processes, improve efficiency, reduce errors, and improve results. While there are many healthcare systems available for clinical labs, only a handful of these systems have been designed specifically for the laboratory setting.

Why use a Lab Information System?

Your lab is busy, and you have no time for a sit-down meeting. Heck, you don't even have the time to go over the data in your LIS. If you were any more swamped, you'd be drowning in all of that paperwork! You need something that will track all of your results without fail.

And once again: why use a Lab Information System?

For starters, it's easier for everyone involved when everything about your lab is in one place. Patients can access their test results on their own time (oh joy), and technicians can easily see the tests performed on patients and what needs to be done next time. Managers can track progress on all fronts—all from within the same program. It makes it much harder for mistakes to slip through the cracks because there aren't any cracks left. A good LIS also integrates with other clinical systems, such as EMRs and pharmacy systems, so that everything stays up-to-date and accurate between departments (which saves valuable hours). And as we've already mentioned above—it's just plain better than having piles upon piles of paper floating around everywhere.

How to choose the right Laboratory Information System

Choosing the right Laboratory Information System (LIS) is no easy task. There are many factors to consider, including:

  • Flexibility and scalability of the system. Will your needs be simple or complex? Will you need to add more users or expand the database in the future?
  • Integration with other systems in your environment, such as patient management software and billing systems.
  • HIPAA compliance—what does this mean for you? Will it make things easier or harder for your staff to use? What data do you have access to and why do you need it? How secure are these files from outside threats like hackers or viruses?

Given all of these considerations, choosing a LIS can seem daunting. But don't worry; we've created a handy flowchart that will help guide you through every step of selecting the right system for your lab! Just consider the below points.

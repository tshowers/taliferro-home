Getting Started

Digital Transformation is a goal that all organizations aim for, and the Covid-19 pandemic has only made it more of a priority.

To successfully transition to a digital business, you need to understand what your current business model looks like and be able to define your target state. You must also have the ability to execute this plan. The following steps can help guide you in making this change:

  • Identify your Business Processes - This first step involves identifying all of the processes within your organization that are business critical and determining how each one works by using BPMN 2.0 diagrams. I recommend using tools such as Visio or Lucidchart, which provide built-in templates for these diagrams, making them easy to create and understand.
  • Automate them with AI - Once we've identified the processes, we can start automating them with Artificial Intelligence (AI). Still, first, we must remember where AI can add value for us to determine how much effort needs to be put into automating them versus manual work outsourced offshore, etc.

However, many organizations struggle with being able to define "Digital Transformation."

You must clearly understand what you want to achieve by moving into the digital world. If your organization hasn't defined what it means by digital transformation, take some time to do so. You can use our Digital Transformation Maturity Model as a guide while describing this aspect of your organization. It will help ensure everyone is on the same page when discussing digital transformation.

Define what you want to achieve by moving into the digital world and how it will impact your customer experience strategy, customer journey, and business processes.

Create an overarching vision for how you want things to look once you've successfully implemented these changes - this informs all other aspects of your plan (e.g., customer experience).

Digital Transformation means different things to different people

Digital transformation is a change in business processes, technology, and operations. It's not a one-size-fits-all approach. The goals of digital transformation are based on the organization's vision and business strategy. Some organizations focus on customer experience. Others focus on cost efficiency or internal integration.

Digital transformation is a continuous process with no fixed time span or deliverables. The aim is to improve continuously while staying aligned with your corporate strategy, so it's essential to ensure your digital system is updated annually or whenever significant changes in the market or technology landscape (e.g., when new channels become available).

Digital transformation is a process and not a goal. It's not just about technology, innovation, or efficiency. It is an essential thing that you can do to help your business succeed in today's digital age.
